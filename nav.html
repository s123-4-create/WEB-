Welcome to Festivals in India

Explore the vibrant culture and traditions of Indian festivals!

Makar-Sankranti

Makar Sankranti is a Hindu festival that celebrates the sun's transition from the Southern to the Northern Hemisphere, marking the end of winter and the beginning of spring. Makar Sankranti is the only Hindu festival based on the solar calendar instead of the lunar.

Diwali

Diwali is a festival of lights celebrated in India that marks the victory of good over evil. It usually takes place in October or November. Deepavali or Diwali is an Indian festival of lights. 'Deepa' means lamp or light and'Vali' means string or row, and Deepavali means rows of lights.

Dussehra

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a major Hindu festival that celebrates the triumph of good over evil. In the Ramayana, Lord Rama defeated the demon king Ravana who had kidnapped his wife, Sita. It is celebrated on the tenth day of the Ashvina month.

Happy Holi

Holi, also known as the festival of colors, is a Hindu celebration that marks the beginning of spring and the triumph of good over evil. Holi is celebrated on the last full moon of the lunar month of Phalguna, which is usually in March.

Durga Pooja

Durga Puja is a 10-day Hindu festival that celebrates the goddess Durga's victory over the demon Mahishasura. It is celebrated in India and by the Bengali diaspora. The festival is a time to worship the goddess.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival that celebrates the birth of the elephant-headed god Ganesha. It is also known as Vinayaka Chaturthi or Vinayaka Chavithi. Ganesh Chaturthi, in Hinduism, 10-day festival marking the birth of the elephant-headed deity Ganesha.

Krishn Janmashtami

Janmashtami is a Hindu festival that celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth incarnation of Lord Vishnu. It is also known as Krishna Janmashtami, Gokulashtami, Shrikrishna Jayanti. Janmashtami is celebrated in many parts of India, including Mathura, Vrindavan, Dwarika.
